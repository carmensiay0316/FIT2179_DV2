International student enrolments in Australia

Mapping where international students study,
where they come from, and what they study

This visualisation explores international student enrolments in Australia.
It compares state concentration, source countries, education sectors,
export income, and student-related migration flows.

Subtopic 1

Where do Australia’s international students come from?

This section shows the top source countries in 2025.
It links each country’s location with its enrolment ranking.

Top source countries and their locations

The map shows where the top source countries are located.
Circle size shows total student enrolments from each country.
The lollipop chart ranks countries by total enrolments.

China and India were the two largest source countries.
Nepal, the Philippines and Vietnam formed the next group.

How to read this chart

The map shows source-country locations.
The lollipop chart ranks enrolment size.

Click a country in either view
to highlight it across both charts.

Subtopic 3

How did education sector enrolments change over time?

This section compares enrolment patterns across education sectors.
The first chart shows how large each sector was over time.
The second chart shows whether sector rankings changed each year.

Sector enrolment volume by education sector, 2019 to 2025

This stacked area chart shows how enrolments changed by sector.
Total enrolments fell sharply between 2020 and 2021.
This reflects disruption during the pandemic-affected period.
Enrolments then recovered strongly from 2022 to 2024.

Higher Education remained the largest sector across the whole period.
Vocational Education and Training was the second largest sector.
English-language courses were smaller, but still changed noticeably.
Schools and Non-award enrolments remained comparatively small.

How to read this chart

The chart highlights both sector size
and overall recovery over time.

Click an area to update the side annotation.
Double-click to clear the selected detail.

Sector ranking over time

This ranking chart compares education sectors by enrolment size each year.
Rank 1 means the sector had the highest enrolments that year.
Lower positions show sectors with smaller enrolment totals.

The chart shows that sector positions changed during this period.
The largest sectors stayed near the top across most years.
Smaller sectors moved between lower ranks as enrolments changed.

This view is useful because it focuses on relative position.
It shows whether a sector became more or less important.

How to read this chart

Rank 1 means the sector had
the highest enrolments in that year.
Lower ranks mean smaller sector totals.

Click a dot or line to show
the selected sector’s ranking details.
Double-click to clear the selection.

Subtopic 5

How did education export income recover by state?

Enrolments show where international students study in Australia.
Export income shows the economic value linked to international education.
This section compares how strongly each state recovered after disruption.

Indexed education export income across states

This figure compares education export income recovery across states.
Each state starts at 100 in 2019–20.
Values below 100 show income below the baseline.
Values above 100 show recovery beyond the baseline.

Most states fell clearly between 2020–21 and 2021–22.
Recovery became clearer from 2022–23 onwards.
By 2023–24, several states exceeded their baseline level.

Western Australia shows the strongest recovery by 2024–25.
New South Wales, Australian Capital Territory and South Australia
also recovered strongly after the disruption period.

Tasmania is the main exception in this figure.
Its index remained below the 2019–20 baseline.
Overall, recovery was uneven across Australian states.

How to read this chart

Read this as recovery speed,
not absolute income size.

Click a line or point to show
the selected state details.
Double-click to clear the annotation.

Subtopic 6

How does export income differ across education sectors?

Student enrolments show the size of international education.
Export income shows how much economic value each sector contributes.

Education export income by sector and financial year

Darker cells indicate higher education export income.
The heatmap compares sector differences across financial years.
Higher Education generated the largest export income overall.

Higher Education fell from A$25.3 billion in 2019–20
to A$13.5 billion in 2021–22.
It then recovered strongly to A$38.8 billion by 2024–25.

Vocational Education and Training was the second largest sector.
It declined during the disruption period,
but recovered to A$10.7 billion by 2024–25.

Smaller sectors contributed much less export income.
The recovery was driven mainly by Higher Education
and Vocational Education and Training.

How to read this chart

Use colour intensity to identify
the strongest income-producing sectors.

Click a cell to show
the selected sector details.
Double-click to clear the annotation.

Source: Australian Government Department of Education.

Subtopic 7

How do enrolments relate to student migration flows?

Enrolment data shows where international students study.
Migration data shows student arrivals and departures across states.

Net student migration by state

Net student migration equals student arrivals minus departures.
Positive values mean student arrivals exceeded student departures.
Negative values mean departures exceeded arrivals.

This map should be read as migration balance, not enrolment size.
A state can have many enrolled students but a smaller net gain.
This depends on both new arrivals and student departures.

The slider shows how the balance changes over financial years.
This helps identify whether a state’s pattern is temporary
or remains consistent across the selected period.

How to read this chart

Positive values show net student gains.
Negative values show net student losses.

Use the slider to compare years.
Double-click to clear the annotation.

Selected state details

Click a state on the map to show its net student migration details here.
This will show the selected state’s net migration balance for the chosen year.
It will also show the total student arrivals and departures that contributed to the balance.

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics, Overseas Migration.
