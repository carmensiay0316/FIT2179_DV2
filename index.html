Mapping the Geography of International Student Enrolments in Australia

This visualisation explores how international student enrolments are distributed across Australian states, source countries and education sectors. It shows that enrolments are concentrated in a few major states, rely heavily on several source countries, and are mainly driven by Higher Education and VET.

Enrolment concentration across states and source countries

The first section introduces the main concentration pattern. International student enrolments in 2025 were concentrated in both a small number of Australian states and a small number of source countries.

Source country concentration

China and India were the two largest source countries for international student enrolments in 2025. Nepal was also a major source country, followed by the Philippines and Vietnam.

*Click a country on the proportional symbol map to highlight it in the ranking and view the annotation,

Australian state concentration

New South Wales and Victoria recorded the largest number of international student enrolments in 2025. Together, they accounted for more than two-thirds of all international student enrolments in the dataset.

The spatial footprint of international student enrolments

The maps translate the state ranking into geography. The choropleth map shows where enrolments are concentrated, while the proportional symbol map makes the size gap between states more visible.

Choropleth map of state enrolments

The choropleth map highlights the east-coast concentration, especially in New South Wales and Victoria, with Queensland forming a second tier.

State enrolments and source-country composition

The map shows the geographic concentration of international student enrolments across Australian states and territories. Hover over a state to update the lollipop chart, which shows the top source countries contributing to that state's enrolments.

This linked view combines geographic overview with details on demand. The map shows where enrolments are concentrated, while the lollipop chart shows which countries contribute most to the hovered state.

Sector composition and ranking changes from 2019 to 2025

The sector charts show how international student enrolments changed over time. Higher Education and VET remained the two largest sectors, while ELICOS was more disrupted during the pandemic period.

Sector enrolment composition over time

The stacked area chart shows the enrolment volume of each education sector from 2019 to 2025. Higher Education and VET dominate the overall pattern.

Sector ranking over time

The bump chart ranks education sectors by enrolments each year. It shows that the largest sectors mostly stayed in the same order across the period.

Regional source markets reveal broader enrolment dependence

Grouping the top source countries into broader regions shows that Australia’s international student enrolments are strongly connected to East Asian, South Asian and Southeast Asian source markets. This view reduces the complexity of the country ranking and highlights broader regional dependence.

Regional source-market waffle chart

Each square represents about one percent of enrolments among the top 20 source countries in 2025. Colour groups those countries into broader source regions.

Export income recovery by state

Enrolment counts show where international students study, but education export income shows the economic value of international education. This view compares how each state's export income changed relative to its 2019–20 level.

Indexed education export income across Australian states

The index is set to 100 in 2019–20 for each state. Values above 100 mean that export income has recovered beyond the 2019–20 level, while values below 100 indicate that export income remained lower than the baseline. Hover over the lines to view each state's exact export income and recovery index.

Export income differs strongly across education sectors

Student enrolments show the size of international education, but export income shows its economic value. This view compares how much income each education sector contributed across financial years.

Education export income by sector and financial year

Darker cells indicate higher export income. The heatmap makes it easier to compare both sector differences and the pandemic-related decline across years.

Higher Education generated the largest export income across all years. Most sectors declined during the pandemic-affected years before recovering after 2022–23.

Source: Australian Government Department of Education, Education export income, financial year.

Student enrolments sit within broader migration flows

The Department of Education data shows where international students study, while the ABS migration data shows how student-related overseas migration movements differ across states and years. This view connects the enrolment story with Australia's temporary migration system.

Student migration balance by state

Net student migration is calculated as student arrivals minus student departures. Orange indicates negative net student migration, while blue indicates positive net student migration. Use the slider to compare financial years from 2004–05 to 2024–25.

The fixed colour scale makes it possible to compare years consistently. Pandemic-affected years show the sharp disruption to student migration, while later years show the recovery in net student migrant gains.

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics, Overseas Migration. Estimates for 2024–25 are preliminary and rounded to the nearest 10.
