Mapping the Influence of International Students Across Australia

International students are concentrated in a few Australian states and come from a small number of major source countries.

A few states and countries dominate the enrolment picture

International student enrolments in 2025 were concentrated in both a small number of Australian states and a small number of source countries.

Australian state concentration

New South Wales and Victoria recorded the largest number of international student enrolments in 2025. Together, they accounted for more than two-thirds of all international student enrolments in the dataset.

Source country concentration

China and India were the two largest source countries for international student enrolments in 2025. Nepal was also a major source country, followed by the Philippines and Vietnam.

The geography of enrolments is concentrated along Australia’s east coast

The map shows that international student enrolments in 2025 were concentrated in New South Wales and Victoria, with Queensland forming a second tier. Western Australia and South Australia also contributed meaningful enrolments, while smaller jurisdictions such as Tasmania, the Northern Territory and the Australian Capital Territory had much lower totals.

Circle size makes the state gap more visible

The proportional symbol map shows the same state-level enrolment pattern in another way. Larger circles appear over New South Wales and Victoria, reinforcing how strongly enrolments are concentrated in the south-east.

Higher Education and VET dominate the sector pattern

Sector enrolments changed unevenly between 2019 and 2025. Higher Education and VET remained the largest sectors, while ELICOS declined sharply during the pandemic period and did not return to its earlier level by 2025.

The largest sectors mostly stayed in the same order

The bump chart ranks education sectors by enrolments each year. Higher Education and VET remained the two largest sectors across the period, while ELICOS dropped during the pandemic period and did not regain its earlier position by 2025.
